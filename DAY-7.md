1. **Keep a "Portfolio Code" folder** — When you write good code, save it with High effort review from Claude. This builds a portfolio piece.

2. **Use Sonnet for explaining YOUR code** — After you write something, ask Claude to explain it clearly. This helps you learn what you wrote.

3. **Escalate strategically** — If Sonnet gives you a solution but you're not confident, ask "Can you explain why this works?" before accepting it.

4. **Resume as a living document** — Start drafting your resume now with Sonnet + High. Each project, update it. By graduation, it's already polished (Opus + High only for final push).

5. **Group research tasks** — Instead of asking about 10 papers one at a time, ask Sonnet to "Compare these 5 papers on X topic" in one go (High effort).

Here's a summary of the key takeaways:

**The biggest concern by far is the 6-day work week with rotational off.** This is not a junior-analyst norm — it's a structural policy that almost guarantees compromised weekends and eventual burnout. Combined with no salary disclosure, this JD asks candidates to take on significant unknowns before getting anything concrete in return.

**The "Tier 1 college preferred" clause** is worth noting specifically. For a role requiring just 1 year of experience and skills that are fully learnable, this filter signals a prestige-conscious culture that may translate into politics and favoritism inside the team.

**On the positive side**, Policybazaar / PB Fintech is a publicly listed, established company — not a fly-by-night startup. The tech stack is genuinely marketable (SQL + Python + Power BI) and the role does involve real analytical work, not just report running.
